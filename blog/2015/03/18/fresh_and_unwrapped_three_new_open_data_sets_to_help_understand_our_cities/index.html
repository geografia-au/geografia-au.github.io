Fresh open data: three new data sets to help understand our cities

As advocates of open data initiatives, we're pleased to discover three freshly released data sets available to planners and economists seeking to understand Victoria's cities. Gaining access to these data sets is hugely important for planning and managing cities. Congratulations to the government departments responsible for compiling and releasing these important data sets - the open data movement is gaining momentum!

1. Detailed commercial, industrial and residential floor space data



Geoscience Australia has worked hard to collect and distribute floor space data for industrial, commercial and residential uses at a small area (SA1) level across Australia. This is the first time (in our knowledge) that floor space data has been released at such a fine grain level. Until now, analysts have relied on very coarse geographical data, such as ABS destination zones or SA2s. These coarse geographies don't capture the intricacies of localised land use differences. GA's data set can be used to measure the spatial distribution of key land uses across the city, or act as a proxy measure for building heights, employment, and job density.

  • Interactive map of industrial areas in Melbourne: http://cdb.io/1AyR701 
  • Source data: http://www.ga.gov.au/scientific-topics/hazards/risk-impact/nexis

2. Property parcels - Victoria wide!



That's all that needs to be said - there are a million potential uses for this dataset. 

  • Source data: https://www.data.vic.gov.au/data/dataset/parcel-view-vicmap-property 

3. Pre-1994 Local Government Boundaries



These old boundaries are fun for the nostalgically inclined. However, they are also really useful when analysing or visualising data from any Census prior to 1996. 

  • Interactive map of Victoria's pre-Kennet LGA boundaries: http://cdb.io/1AySUSX 
  • Source data: https://www.data.vic.gov.au/data/dataset/local-government-areas-for-victoria-prior-to-the-1994-amalg...
